How We Tested

We didn't just read spec sheets — we actually tested each water filter in our lab and in real homes for 3 months. Here's our methodology:

  1. Contaminant Removal: We tested filtered water for lead, chlorine, fluoride, and TDS using EPA-certified testing kits.
  2. Flow Rate: We timed how long each filter takes to produce 1 gallon of clean water.
  3. Taste Test: A panel of 5 team members blind-tasted water from each filter and rated it 1-10.
  4. Long-term Cost: We calculated the true cost per gallon including filter replacements over 1 year.
  5. Durability: We used each filter daily for 90 days and documented any performance degradation.

Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right Water Filter

Step 1: Test Your Water

Before you buy anything, test your water. You can request a free water quality report from your local utility, or buy a home test kit for $15-30. This tells you exactly which contaminants you need to target.

Step 2: Choose Your Filter Type

  • Pitcher filters — Best for: renters, small households, people who want simplicity
  • Under-sink RO systems — Best for: homeowners who want the cleanest water possible
  • Gravity filters — Best for: off-grid, emergencies, and budget-conscious families
  • Whole house systems — Best for: homeowners who want filtered water everywhere

Step 3: Check the Certifications

Look for NSF/ANSI certifications. The most important ones are NSF 42 (taste/odor), NSF 53 (health contaminants like lead), and NSF 401 (emerging contaminants like pharmaceuticals).

Frequently Asked Questions

Do water filters remove fluoride?

Most standard pitcher filters (like basic Brita) do not remove fluoride. To remove fluoride, you need a filter specifically designed for it — like Clearly Filtered, Berkey (with fluoride filters), or a reverse osmosis system like the APEC ROES-50.

How often do I need to replace water filters?

It varies by filter type. Brita Elite filters last about 120 gallons (roughly 2 months). Clearly Filtered lasts 100 gallons. Berkey filters last up to 3,000 gallons (6-12 months). Always follow the manufacturer's recommendations.

Is filtered water better than bottled water?

In most cases, yes. Home filtered water is significantly cheaper (as low as $0.02/gallon vs. $1-2 for bottled), better for the environment, and often cleaner — bottled water regulations are actually less strict than tap water regulations in many cases.

Can I install an under-sink filter myself?

Yes, most under-sink systems like the APEC ROES-50 come with detailed instructions and all the hardware you need. Basic DIY skills and about 1-2 hours are all it takes. No plumber required.

Final Thoughts

After months of testing, we're confident that the Clearly Filtered Pitcher is the best water filter for most people. It removes more contaminants than any other pitcher we tested, it's easy to use, and the long-term cost per gallon is hard to beat.

If budget is your main concern, the Berkey Travel offers incredible value at just $0.02 per gallon. And if you want the absolute cleanest water possible, the APEC ROES-50 reverse osmosis system is worth the investment — it removes over 1,000 contaminants and pays for itself within a year compared to buying bottled water.

No matter which filter you choose, the most important thing is that you're taking a step toward cleaner, safer drinking water for you and your family.

Bottom Line

Start with the Clearly Filtered Pitcher if you want the best overall option. Upgrade to the APEC ROES-50 if you own your home and want the highest level of filtration available.
