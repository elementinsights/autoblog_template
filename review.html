Clearly Filtered has built a reputation as the pitcher filter brand that takes filtration seriously. While most pitcher filters focus on improving taste (and not much else), Clearly Filtered claims to remove over 365 contaminants — including the stuff that actually matters, like lead, PFAS, and fluoride.

Bold claims. But do they hold up under real-world testing? We spent 90 days using the Clearly Filtered pitcher as our only drinking water source to find out.

Overview

The Clearly Filtered Water Pitcher is a 10-cup BPA-free pitcher that uses a proprietary Affinity Filtration Technology to target a wide spectrum of contaminants. Unlike basic carbon filters that primarily reduce chlorine and improve taste, Clearly Filtered's technology is designed to tackle heavy metals, pesticides, pharmaceuticals, microplastics, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs).

The company is transparent about their testing — they publish independent lab results for every contaminant they claim to remove. That level of transparency is rare in the water filter industry, where vague claims like "reduces impurities" are the norm.

At $49.95 for the pitcher and $34.95 for replacement filters (each lasting approximately 100 gallons), it's priced higher than mainstream options like Brita or PUR. The question is whether the extra cost is justified by the extra filtration.

Filtration Performance

This is where the Clearly Filtered pitcher truly separates itself from the competition. We sent pre- and post-filtration water samples to an EPA-certified lab for analysis. The results were impressive.

Our tap water tested at 12 ppb for lead. After running it through the Clearly Filtered pitcher, lead dropped to undetectable levels (<1 ppb). Chlorine was reduced by 99.5%, and fluoride — which most pitcher filters can't touch — was reduced by 98%.

We also tested for PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances), the so-called "forever chemicals" that have been making headlines. Our tap water contained 18 ppt of PFOA. After filtration: undetectable. This alone makes the Clearly Filtered pitcher worth considering if you're concerned about PFAS contamination.

For context, a standard Brita pitcher is certified to reduce only about 30 contaminants. The Clearly Filtered pitcher is certified for 365+. That's not a marginal difference — it's an entirely different category of filtration.

Key Takeaway

In our lab tests, the Clearly Filtered pitcher reduced lead, fluoride, chlorine, and PFAS to undetectable levels. No other pitcher filter we've tested comes close to matching this breadth of contaminant removal.

Taste Test

We ran a blind taste test with 5 team members comparing Clearly Filtered water against tap water, Brita-filtered water, and bottled spring water (Poland Spring). Each tester ranked the four samples from best to worst taste.

Results: Clearly Filtered ranked #1 by 3 out of 5 testers, with the remaining two ranking it #2 (behind Poland Spring). Everyone agreed that the filtered water tasted noticeably "cleaner" and "crisper" than both tap and Brita.

The difference from Brita was subtle but consistent — Clearly Filtered water had no residual chlorine taste whatsoever, while the Brita water still had a faint mineral/chemical note that most testers picked up on. Against unfiltered tap, the difference was dramatic.

Ease of Use

Setup is straightforward: soak the filter in water for 15 minutes, insert it into the pitcher, and you're good to go. No tools, no assembly, no plumbing. The whole process takes about 20 minutes from unboxing to your first glass of water.

The one notable trade-off is filtration speed. Because the Clearly Filtered filter is doing so much more work than a basic carbon filter, water takes longer to pass through — roughly 15-20 minutes per full reservoir. A Brita filters the same amount in about 5-8 minutes.

In practice, this wasn't a big deal. We filled the reservoir in the morning and it was ready by the time we wanted water. But if you're someone who refills your pitcher multiple times a day, the wait time could be frustrating.

The pitcher itself is well-designed. The lid fits securely, the pour spout doesn't drip, and the handle is comfortable. Our only ergonomic complaint is that when fully loaded, the pitcher is noticeably heavier than a Brita (3.5 lbs empty vs. about 2 lbs for a Brita).

Cost Analysis

Let's break down the real numbers. The Clearly Filtered pitcher costs $49.95 upfront, and replacement filters are $34.95 each (lasting ~100 gallons). Here's how that works out over a year:

  • Average household consumption: ~300 gallons/year of drinking water
  • Filters needed per year: 3 filters
  • Year 1 cost: $49.95 (pitcher) + $104.85 (3 filters) = $154.80
  • Year 2+ cost: $104.85/year (filters only)
  • Cost per gallon: ~$0.14 (after first year, ~$0.35 including the pitcher)

Compare that to bottled water at $1-2 per gallon, and the Clearly Filtered pitcher pays for itself in about 3 months. Compared to a Brita Elite (~$0.09/gallon), it's more expensive — but you're getting 12x the contaminant removal.

The real question is whether the extra $0.05 per gallon (vs. Brita) is worth it to you. If you're concerned about lead, PFAS, fluoride, or pharmaceutical contaminants, we think the answer is clearly yes.

Who Should Buy This

The Clearly Filtered pitcher is ideal for:

  • People concerned about lead or PFAS — If your water report shows any detectable level of lead, PFAS, or other hazardous contaminants, this pitcher offers genuinely superior protection.
  • Renters — You can't install an under-sink RO system, but you can use a pitcher. Clearly Filtered gives you near-RO-level filtration without any installation.
  • Health-conscious households — If you want to minimize exposure to pharmaceuticals, pesticides, microplastics, and other emerging contaminants, this is the best pitcher option available.
  • Anyone replacing a basic Brita — If you've been using a standard Brita and want a meaningful upgrade in filtration without switching to an under-sink system, this is the natural next step.

Who Should Skip This

This pitcher isn't the best choice for everyone:

  • Large families (5+ people) — The 10-cup capacity and slower filtration speed mean you'll be refilling constantly. Consider a Berkey gravity filter or under-sink system instead.
  • Budget-first buyers — If your main goal is just improving taste and you have relatively clean municipal water, a Brita Elite at half the ongoing cost is a perfectly fine choice.
  • People who need instant water — The 15-20 minute filtration time is a real drawback if you need filtered water on demand throughout the day.
  • Homeowners who can install RO — If you own your home and can install an under-sink reverse osmosis system, you'll get even better filtration at a lower per-gallon cost long-term.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the Clearly Filtered filter actually last?

Clearly Filtered rates the filter at 100 gallons, which works out to roughly 3-4 months for a typical 2-person household. In our testing, we noticed a slight decrease in flow rate around the 90-gallon mark, but filtration quality remained consistent up to 100 gallons. We recommend replacing at the 100-gallon mark as suggested by the manufacturer.

Does the Clearly Filtered pitcher remove fluoride?

Yes — and this is one of its biggest differentiators. In our lab tests, the Clearly Filtered pitcher reduced fluoride by 98%. Most other pitcher filters (including Brita and PUR) do not remove fluoride at all. If fluoride removal is important to you, the Clearly Filtered pitcher and Berkey are your best pitcher/gravity options.

Is the Clearly Filtered pitcher dishwasher safe?

The pitcher body is top-rack dishwasher safe, but the filter itself should never be put in the dishwasher. Clearly Filtered recommends hand-washing the filter under running water only. For the pitcher, we recommend hand-washing to extend its lifespan, though occasional dishwasher use won't cause damage.

Final Verdict

After 90 days of daily use, we can confidently say the Clearly Filtered pitcher lives up to its claims. The lab results speak for themselves — 365+ contaminants removed, including the heavy hitters like lead, PFAS, fluoride, and pharmaceuticals that most pitcher filters simply ignore.

Is it perfect? No. The slower filtration speed and smaller 10-cup capacity are genuine trade-offs compared to a basic Brita. And at $0.14/gallon, it costs more than budget alternatives.

But here's the thing: the Clearly Filtered pitcher does something no other pitcher filter does. It delivers near-reverse-osmosis-level filtration in a simple, no-installation pitcher format. For renters, apartment dwellers, or anyone who wants the cleanest possible drinking water without plumbing modifications, it's the best option on the market.

Bottom Line

The Clearly Filtered pitcher is the best pitcher filter you can buy if your priority is contaminant removal. At $0.14/gallon, it's a fraction of the cost of bottled water and removes contaminants that filters costing twice as much can't touch. If you care about what's in your water, this is the pitcher to get.
