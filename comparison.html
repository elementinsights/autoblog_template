Side-by-Side Specs

Let's break down the raw specifications before we get into our hands-on findings. Both pitchers hold 10 cups and use proprietary replacement filters, but that's about where the similarities end.

  • Brita Elite: Uses an activated carbon + ion exchange filter. NSF-certified for Standards 42, 53, and 401. Removes chlorine taste, lead, some pharmaceuticals, and about 30 total contaminants.
  • Clearly Filtered: Uses a proprietary Affinity Filtration Technology with multiple media layers. Independently tested against 365+ contaminants including fluoride, PFAS, microplastics, heavy metals, and pesticides.

On paper, the Clearly Filtered specs are significantly more impressive — but specs only tell part of the story. Let's look at how they performed in our real-world testing.

Filtration Performance

This is where Clearly Filtered absolutely dominates. We sent filtered water samples from both pitchers to an independent lab for analysis, and the results weren't even close.

The Brita Elite did a solid job reducing chlorine taste and odor (99% reduction), lead (99%), and mercury (96%). For most people on clean municipal water, this level of filtration is perfectly adequate — it handles the most common tap water concerns well.

The Clearly Filtered pitcher, however, operates on a completely different level. In addition to everything Brita removes, it also targets fluoride (98% reduction), PFAS/forever chemicals (99.7%), microplastics (99.9%), glyphosate (99.7%), and hundreds of other contaminants that Brita's technology simply isn't designed to address.

If you're on well water, live near agricultural land, or are specifically concerned about fluoride or PFAS, Clearly Filtered is the clear winner. If your main concern is basic chlorine taste and lead, Brita does the job at a lower price.

Taste Test

We assembled a 5-person blind taste panel and had them rate water from each pitcher (plus unfiltered tap water as a control) on a 1-10 scale. The results:

  • Unfiltered Tap: 5.2/10 average
  • Brita Elite: 8.0/10 average — panelists noted clean, crisp water with no chlorine taste
  • Clearly Filtered: 9.0/10 average — panelists described it as "noticeably smoother" and "almost like spring water"

Both pitchers produced water that tasted significantly better than tap. The difference between them was subtle but consistent — 4 out of 5 panelists preferred the Clearly Filtered water when tasting them back to back. The fifth panelist rated them equally.

Filter Life & Cost

This is where Brita pulls ahead. The numbers are straightforward:

  • Brita Elite: Each filter lasts ~120 gallons. Replacement filters cost ~$10.50 each, working out to roughly $0.09 per gallon. For a family of four drinking 1 gallon/day, that's about $33/year in filter costs.
  • Clearly Filtered: Each filter lasts ~100 gallons. Replacement filters cost ~$14 each, working out to roughly $0.14 per gallon. For the same family, that's about $51/year in filter costs.

Brita wins on cost by a meaningful margin — about $18/year less for a typical household. However, when you consider the vastly superior contaminant removal of Clearly Filtered, many buyers will find the extra cost per gallon well worth it.

Ease of Use

The Brita Elite is about as simple as water filtration gets. The filter snaps in with a twist-lock mechanism, the electronic indicator tells you when to replace it, and the flow rate is excellent — filling a full pitcher takes about 4-5 minutes.

The Clearly Filtered pitcher is slightly more involved. The filter requires a 24-hour initial soak before first use (Brita needs just a 15-second rinse). The flow rate is also slower — expect about 15-20 minutes for a full pitcher. There's no electronic indicator; you track replacement based on gallon usage.

Neither pitcher is complicated, but Brita wins on convenience. The faster flow rate alone makes a noticeable difference in daily use, especially if you're frequently refilling throughout the day.

Design & Build Quality

Both pitchers are made from BPA-free plastic and hold approximately 10 cups of filtered water. The Brita Elite has a slimmer profile that fits easily in most refrigerator doors, with a comfortable grip and easy-pour lid.

The Clearly Filtered pitcher is slightly bulkier due to the larger filter housing. The build quality feels marginally more premium — the lid has a tighter seal and the reservoir sits more securely in the pitcher body. However, it's a close call and unlikely to sway anyone's purchasing decision on its own.

Who Should Choose Brita Elite

The Brita Elite is the better choice if:

  • You're on a tight budget and want the most affordable filtered water possible
  • You have relatively clean municipal water and mainly want to improve taste
  • You want the fastest flow rate and easiest filter replacement
  • Lead and chlorine are your primary concerns (Brita handles both well)
  • You prefer a "set it and forget it" approach with the electronic replacement indicator

Who Should Choose Clearly Filtered

The Clearly Filtered pitcher is the better choice if:

  • You want the most thorough contaminant removal available in a pitcher filter
  • You're specifically concerned about fluoride, PFAS, microplastics, or pesticides
  • You have well water or live in an area with known water quality issues
  • You have young children and want to minimize exposure to emerging contaminants
  • You're willing to pay a small premium ($0.05/gallon more) for significantly better filtration
Key Takeaway

Brita Elite is a solid, affordable filter for everyday use. Clearly Filtered is the better investment if you care about what's actually in your water beyond chlorine taste — it removes 12x more contaminants at only $0.05 more per gallon.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Brita remove fluoride from water?

No. The Brita Elite filter is not designed to remove fluoride. If fluoride removal is important to you, Clearly Filtered is one of the few pitcher filters that can reduce fluoride by up to 98%. Reverse osmosis systems also remove fluoride effectively.

How long do Clearly Filtered filters actually last?

Clearly Filtered rates their filters at 100 gallons, which translates to roughly 2-4 months for a typical household. In our testing, we found the filter maintained excellent performance through the full 100 gallons. Some users report reduced flow rate near the end of the filter's life, which is normal and indicates the filter is still working.

Is it worth paying more for Clearly Filtered over Brita?

It depends on your priorities. If you mainly want to remove chlorine taste and basic contaminants, Brita does the job well at a lower cost. But if you want protection against a wider range of contaminants — especially fluoride, PFAS, and microplastics — the extra $0.05 per gallon for Clearly Filtered is a worthwhile investment. That works out to roughly $18 more per year for a family of four.

Final Thoughts

Both Brita Elite and Clearly Filtered are excellent water filters — but they serve different needs. Brita is the practical, budget-friendly choice that does a great job with common contaminants. Clearly Filtered is the performance-driven choice for people who want the highest level of filtration available without installing an under-sink system.

After 90 days of side-by-side testing, our team unanimously agreed: if you can afford the small price premium, Clearly Filtered is the better pitcher. The gap in contaminant removal is simply too large to ignore, and the taste difference — while subtle — is real.

That said, if you're currently drinking unfiltered tap water and need an affordable entry point, you absolutely cannot go wrong with the Brita Elite. Any filtration is better than no filtration.

Bottom Line

Clearly Filtered wins this comparison with our overall rating of 9.6/10 vs. Brita's 8.5/10. It removes 365+ contaminants (vs. 30), tastes better in blind testing, and costs only $0.05 more per gallon. For most people, it's worth the upgrade.
